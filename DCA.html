📝 What I learned & practice shift

Before this course, I treated figures as visual summaries that required textual support. The feedback taught me that digital literacy in science means building standalone visual evidence. I now start every R plotting task by asking: “Can a busy clinician grasp the result in three seconds?” This habit of embedding statistical clarity directly into graphics has become a signature of my research work, transforming how I communicate data—from code to conclusion.

⚡ Outcome impact: During a subsequent research internship, I applied this principle to generate R‑based summary tables with all key statistics embedded inline. The tables were so clear that the principal investigator adopted them directly into a conference poster, saving revision time and earning me a commendation for “exceptional data presentation.”
